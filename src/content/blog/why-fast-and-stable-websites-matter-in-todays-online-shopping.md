<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why Website Speed Is Important for New AI Shopping</strong></h2>

<p>Online shopping is changing fast. Now, autonomous agents, called AI agents, can shop for people. These helpers find, compare, and buy products all by themselves. This is called agentic commerce.</p>

<div class="wp-block-uagb-image alignleft uagb-block-a5b836df wp-block-uagb-image--layout-default wp-block-uagb-image--effect-static wp-block-uagb-image--align-left"><figure class="wp-block-uagb-image__figure"><img srcset="/images/blog/bar-char-illustrating-average-mobile-load-times.webp ,/images/blog/bar-char-illustrating-average-mobile-load-times.webp 780w, /images/blog/bar-char-illustrating-average-mobile-load-times.webp 360w" sizes="(max-width: 480px) 150px" src="/images/blog/bar-char-illustrating-average-mobile-load-times.webp" alt="Average Mobile Load Time by Performance Grade" class="uag-image-1773" width="678" height="470" title="bar-char-illustrating-average-mobile load-times" loading="lazy" role="img"/></figure></div>

<p>For these AI helpers to work well, websites need to be fast and stable. If a website is slow or keeps breaking, the AI helpers might not show its products to shoppers. This means the website could lose visitors and sales. Experts like Moz and Search Engine Journal say it’s very important to make websites work well for both people and AI helpers (Moz Blog, Search Engine Journal).</p>

<p>Also, search tools on websites help shoppers find what they want quickly. But these tools only work well if the website is fast and reliable. Studies show people who use good search on a website are more likely to buy something (Neil Patel Blog, Smashing Magazine).</p>

<p>So, having a fast and steady website is not just a tech thing—it’s very important for business success today.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Problem: Many Websites Are Too Slow</strong></h2>

<p>Even though speed is so important, many websites are still slow or have problems. Slow websites make visitors unhappy and can cause them to leave. This also hurts how well the website shows up in search results.</p>

<p>What We Found</p>

<p>We analyzed the front page of 481 websites. Here’s what we saw:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong>Failing Performance </strong>(Grade F): 272 websites (over 56%) received a failing grade, indicating serious issues that negatively impact user experience and SEO.</li>

<li><strong>Top Performance</strong> (Grade A): Only 21 websites (about 4%) achieved the highest grade, reflecting excellent speed and optimization.</li>

<li><strong>Moderate Performance</strong> (Grades B, C, D):</li>
</ul>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>45 websites scored a B, showing good performance but with room for improvement.</li>

<li>71 websites scored a C, indicating average performance.</li>

<li>72 websites scored a D, reflecting poor performance that requires attention.</li>
</ul>

<p>This shows many websites need to get faster and better.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>The Invisible 5% of the Web</strong></h2>

<p>While running the analysis, we noticed something surprising. A small but stubborn group of websites refused to load—no matter how long we waited. After 120 seconds, our system automatically moved on. About 5% of all URLs failed this way. It was like watching doors that never opened, no matter how many times you knocked.</p>

<p>Now, imagine an AI system like GPT or an autonomous shopping agent encountering those same sites. It wouldn’t wait around either—it would simply skip them and move on to faster, more accessible options.</p>

<p>In other words, for these sites, it’s not just that humans never see them—<strong>AI won’t either.</strong> And in a future where discovery and decision-making depend on AI agents, that’s the digital equivalent of locking your store doors during business hours.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Make Your Website Faster</strong></h3>

<p>Here are some easy ways to make your website better:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Clean up your code: Remove any extra CSS or JavaScript that you don’t need.</li>

<li>Fix slow-loading parts: Make sure important styles load fast and delay the less important ones.</li>

<li>Make images smaller: Use modern formats like WebP to keep images light.</li>

<li>Speed up your server: Use caching and content delivery networks (CDNs) to make your site load faster.</li>

<li>Keep checking: Use tools like WarpInsights regularly to find and fix new problems.</li>
</ul>
