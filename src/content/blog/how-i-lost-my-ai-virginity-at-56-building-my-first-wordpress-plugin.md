<h3 class="wp-block-heading">A practical beginner’s reflections to mixing AI with WordPress — no midlife crisis required.</h3>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Introduction – <em>Confessions of an AI Late Bloomer</em></h4>

<p>At 56, I never thought I’d be diving into artificial intelligence. I’ve watched technology change — dial-up internet, smartphones, cloud everything — but AI always felt like it belonged to coders in Silicon Valley.</p>

<p>Then one day, curiosity got the better of me. I started small:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>asking AI to answer questions,</li>

<li>then to clean up my documentation,</li>

<li>then to fix tricky syntax (hello, Regular Expressions).</li>
</ul>

<p>Soon enough, it was writing functions for me. Then I thought: <em>why not a plugin?</em></p>

<p>The goal wasn’t just curiosity. After 25 years of memorizing syntax across endless languages, I was tired of “traditional learning.” I wanted to see if AI could help me deliver the <strong class="">same quality code</strong> to my clients — faster and cheaper.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img src="/images/blog/b54ecfde-cb56-44d4-a99f-40fd45b2f9b4-1024x576.webp" alt="How I Lost My AI Virginity at 56: Building My First WordPress Plugin” — with a retro bar and neon. Robots flying around, and a guy in front of a computer." class="wp-image-1732"/><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">How I Lost My AI Virginity at 56: Building My First WordPress Plugin”</figcaption></figure>

<p>Not everyone agrees with this approach, and that’s fine. But this was my experiment. </p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Section 1: Rules of Engagement</h4>

<p>Here’s how I approached using AI with clients:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong class="">Be honest.</strong> Always tell your client you’ll be using AI, and explain how it benefits <em>them.</em></li>

<li><strong class="">Pass on the savings.</strong> If a plugin takes 20 hours normally but 5 with AI, don’t bill for 20.</li>

<li><strong class="">Don’t get lazy.</strong> AI is a helper, not a boss. If it spits out code you don’t fully understand — learn it.</li>

<li><strong class="">Hourly rate?</strong> Still up for debate. Should you bill your full rate, or a discount? To explain my thinking, here’s a short story…</li>
</ul>

<h5 class="wp-block-heading">The $100 Fix: A Story About Knowing Your Price</h5>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote" style="margin-top:0;margin-right:var(--wp--preset--spacing--40);margin-bottom:0;margin-left:var(--wp--preset--spacing--40);padding-right:var(--wp--preset--spacing--60);padding-left:var(--wp--preset--spacing--60)"><p class="has-small-font-size">A BMW breaks down on the side of the road. The owner — frustrated and waiting — is approached by a passing BMW mechanic.</p>

<p class="has-small-font-size">The mechanic takes a quick look, pulls out a screwdriver, turns one screw, and suddenly the car purrs to life.</p>

<p class="has-small-font-size">“Thanks!” the owner says. “How much do I owe you?”</p>

<p class="has-small-font-size">“One hundred dollars,” replies the mechanic.</p>

<p class="has-small-font-size">“Wait, $100? For turning a screw? That’s too much.”</p>

<p class="has-small-font-size">The mechanic smiles:<br>“Turning the screw is free. Knowing <em>which</em> screw to turn? That’s what costs $100.”</p>
</blockquote>

<p><strong>That’s the heart of it. Clients aren’t paying for keystrokes — they’re paying for expertise.</strong></p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Section 2: Why WordPress + AI Is the Perfect First Date</h4>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong class="">WordPress familiarity:</strong> Most of us already know plugins. They’re just add-ons that expand your site. </li>

<li><strong class="">AI accessibility:</strong> Tools like ChatGPT or Claude let anyone try AI, no advanced degree required. </li>

<li><strong class="">Together:</strong> AI + WordPress opens doors: content generators, SEO helpers, chatbots — even tools that <em>you</em> invent.</li>
</ul>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Section 3: Facing the Fears (and Excuses)</h4>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><em>“I’m too old for this.”</em> Nope. AI tools are actually designed to simplify.</li>

<li><em>“I don’t know how the new fashionable language or technology works.”</em> Neither did I. AI now helps you <strong class="">write</strong> the code.</li>

<li><em>“It’s too complex.”</em> Start small. My first plugin wasn’t revolutionary — but it worked. And I kept iterating.</li>
</ul>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Section 4 My First Plugin Attempt</h4>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li><strong class="">The Idea:</strong> A plugin that integrate with a ticketing systems and allow the user to create, maintain, and view tickets.</li>

<li><strong class="">Step 1:</strong> I asked an AI tool to write the skeleton code for a WordPress plugin.</li>

<li><strong class="">Step 2:</strong> I Created the files and code that AI suggested.</li>

<li><strong class="">Step 3:</strong> I Installed Plugin and activated plugin.</li>

<li><strong class="">Result:</strong> It did not work… It did no even activated. I looked at the code some of the code was missing or made no sense.</li>
</ul>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Section 5: Lessons I Learned the Hard Way</h4>

<ol class="wp-block-list"><li><strong class="">AI is your collaborator, not your replacement.</strong> Do not expect magic.</li>

<li><strong class="">Errors are teachers.</strong> I learned more by asking “What went wrong?” than when things worked.</li>

<li><strong class="">Context is everything.</strong> Be specific in your prompts:<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Ask for suggested file structure.</li>

<li>Get steps in the right order.</li>

<li>Generate one file at a time (main file, admin menu, API client, widgets).</li>

<li>Test after each change.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><strong class="">Use Git. Often.</strong> AI sometimes rewrites code unexpectedly. With commits, you can roll back before your work disappears.<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Without commits? One slip and you’ll wish you were at the dentist getting a root canal instead.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion – <em>A New Kind of First Time</em></h4>

<p>Losing my “AI virginity” wasn’t about nailing it on the first try. It was about saying yes to trying, failing, adjusting, and trying again.</p>

<p>Today, I’ve got a semi functional plugin on my dashboard — built with AI. And I’m not stopping there.</p>

<p>If I can start this journey at 56, so can you.  More to come.</p>
