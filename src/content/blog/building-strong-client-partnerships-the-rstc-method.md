<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="introduction-building-our-special-client-tower-3-m">Introduction: Building Our Special Client Tower! (3 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Speaker Notes:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Imagine we’re building a big, strong tower together-one that helps us become great partners with our clients.</li>

<li>This tower uses the&nbsp;<strong>RSTC Method</strong>-four building blocks stacked on top of each other.</li>

<li>Think of it like the Golden Rule: treat clients how you want to be treated.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="block-1-respect--the-foundation-6-min">Block 1: Respect – The Foundation (6 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Respect is the first and most important block.</li>

<li>It means caring about the client’s time, knowledge, and opinions.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>How to Show Respect:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Be on time and prepared.</li>

<li>Listen carefully-don’t interrupt.</li>

<li>Ask smart questions about their business.</li>

<li>Be honest: if you don’t know something, say so and promise to find out.</li>

<li>Keep promises, even small ones.</li>

<li>Use clear, simple language-explain technical terms.</li>

<li>Always be polite and professional.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Personal Example:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>I’m good at listening and waiting to respond, which helps me understand clients’ needs.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="block-2-safety--the-other-foundation-6-min">Block 2: Safety – The Other Foundation (6 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Safety is just as important as respect.</li>

<li>Clients need to feel safe and in control-they can say “no” at any time.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>How to Build Safety:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Make it clear they’re in charge-no pressure.</li>

<li>Offer choices and let them decide at their own pace.</li>

<li>Be transparent about costs, timelines, and next steps.</li>

<li>Share information before they ask-no surprises.</li>

<li>Care more about solving their problem than making a sale.</li>

<li>Keep their information private.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Personal Example:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>I naturally wait for the client to lead, which makes them feel safe and respected.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="block-3-trust--building-higher-6-min">Block 3: Trust – Building Higher (6 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Trust grows when respect and safety are in place.</li>

<li>Clients trust you when you’re reliable, skilled, and genuinely want to help.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>How to Build Trust:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Always do what you say you’ll do.</li>

<li>Finish work on time, or communicate early if there’s a delay.</li>

<li>Show your expertise through your work, not just words.</li>

<li>Admit when you don’t know something.</li>

<li>Communicate openly-share updates and explain your decisions.</li>

<li>Handle mistakes professionally and honestly.</li>

<li>Be consistent-act the same way every time.</li>

<li>Put the client’s needs first, even if it means less work for you.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Personal Example:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>My gut feeling helps me know when a project is a good fit, so I only take on work I believe in.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="the-top-real-client-partnership-3-min">The Top: Real Client Partnership (3 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>With respect, safety, and trust, you reach the top: a true partnership.</li>

<li>Now you’re working together as a team, not just doing a job.</li>

<li>You can tackle tough problems, brainstorm new ideas, and celebrate successes together.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="listening-for-ready-signals-3-min">Listening for “Ready!” Signals (3 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Once trust grows, listen for signs the client is ready to move forward.</li>

<li>Signs include questions about getting started, costs, or bringing in decision-makers.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>What to Do:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Gently check if they’re ready: “How would you like to move forward?”</li>

<li>If yes, show them the next steps clearly and keep things safe and comfortable.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="handling-not-ready-or-no-3-min">Handling “Not Ready” or “No” (3 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Sometimes the answer is “no” or “not yet”-that’s okay!</li>

<li>Respect their choice-never push or argue.</li>

<li>If they’re willing, ask why, so you can learn.</li>

<li>Stay polite and thank them for their time.</li>

<li>If it’s “not yet,” ask when might be a better time to check in.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="keeping-in-touch-nurturing-the-relationship-2-min">Keeping in Touch: Nurturing the Relationship (2 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>If there’s no clear yes or no, keep in touch in a helpful, friendly way (not pushy).</li>

<li>Send a thank-you email after your talk.</li>

<li>Share helpful articles or tips every few weeks.</li>

<li>After three months, check in gently-no pressure.</li>

<li>Always trust your gut about when to reach out.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="using-the-right-words-1-min">Using the Right Words (1 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Use words that show you care, listen, and let them choose.</li>

<li>Avoid words that rush, pressure, or make them feel uncomfortable.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Examples:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>“Based on what you said, here’s an idea…”</li>

<li>“No pressure-take your time deciding.”</li>

<li>“Just wanted to update you…”</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="conclusion-the-rstc-tower-in-action-1-min">Conclusion: The RSTC Tower in Action (1 min)</h2>

<p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Building strong client partnerships is like building a tower: Respect + Safety + Trust = Partnership.</li>

<li>When you use the RSTC Method, everyone wins-you, your clients, and their business.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Call to Action:</strong></p>

<ul class="wp-block-list"><li>Let’s build more towers together-one block at a time!</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="qa-optional-last-2-3-min">Q&amp;A (Optional, last 2-3 min)</h2>

</ul>

<li>Share a quick personal story for each block.</li>

<li>Pause for short questions after each section.</li>

<li>Keep your tone friendly and conversational.</li>
</ul>
